Understanding Sample Ratio Mismatches

What is a Sample Ratio Mismatch?

A sample ratio mismatch occurs when the actual traffic split between variants differs significantly from the intended 50/50 split.

Why This Matters

Sample Ratio Mismatches Indicate:

  • • Allocation problems - Traffic routing issues in your experiment platform
  • • Technical failures - Bugs in the experiment assignment logic
  • • Biased assignment - Non-random distribution of users
  • • Data integrity issues - Compromised experiment validity

Common Causes

Technical Issues

  • • Experiment platform bugs
  • • Incorrect traffic allocation settings
  • • Caching issues
  • • CDN or load balancer problems

Design Issues

  • • Non-random user assignment
  • • Geographic or demographic bias
  • • Time-based allocation problems
  • • User behavior differences

Statistical Impact

How it affects your results:

  • • Biased estimates - Results may not represent true population effects
  • • Invalid statistical tests - Assumptions of equal allocation are violated
  • • Reduced power - Less efficient use of your sample size
  • • Questionable validity - Results may not be generalizable

What To Do

1. Investigate the Cause

Check your experiment platform settings, traffic allocation logic, and any recent changes to the system.

2. Fix the Allocation

Correct the traffic allocation settings to ensure proper 50/50 split going forward.

3. Consider Restarting

If the mismatch is severe, consider stopping the current experiment and restarting with proper allocation.

4. Document the Issue

Record what caused the mismatch to prevent similar issues in future experiments.
