Understanding Sample Size Warnings

What is a Sample Size Warning?

A sample size warning occurs when your experiment doesn't have enough data to achieve the statistical power needed for reliable results.

Why This Matters

Insufficient Sample Size Leads To:

  • • Low statistical power - High chance of missing real effects
  • • Unreliable results - High risk of false negatives
  • • Wasted resources - Running experiments that can't detect meaningful differences
  • • Poor decision making - Basing business decisions on underpowered data

How Sample Size is Calculated

Sample size depends on:

  • • Effect size - The minimum difference you want to detect
  • • Statistical power - Probability of detecting a real effect (typically 80%)
  • • Significance level - Risk of false positives (typically 5%)
  • • Base conversion rate - The expected conversion rate

What To Do

1. Extend the Experiment

Run the experiment longer to collect more data and reach the required sample size.

2. Increase Traffic

If possible, increase the number of visitors to your experiment to reach the required sample size faster.

3. Reconsider the Effect Size

If the required sample size is too large, consider whether you can detect a larger effect size instead.

4. Accept the Risk

If extending isn't possible, understand that your results have lower statistical power and higher risk of false negatives.
